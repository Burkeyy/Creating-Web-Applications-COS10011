Canvas - What is it?

Canvas is a cloud-based learning management system (LMS). Learning management systems are software applications typically used in a corporate or educational market. In the case of Canvas, it is a streamlined method of administering online courses, making education easier to access by students, whether itâ€™s for learning purposes or for feedback on their efforts. For educators and teachers, Canvas allows for a simpler and more effective way of managing the course for their students.

Features

  • Discussion boards for asynchronous communication
  • Chat rooms where students and teachers can communicate live
  • Ability to share key updates or results on the fly
  • Implementation of Quizzes
  • Recording and uploading of audio and/or video
  • Analytics and efficient data gathering
  • Myriad tools for educators to customize course content

Competitor Comparison

Canvas Google Classroom
Pros
  • More suited for higher education
  • Powerful gradebook management
  • Integrates well with third-party applications
  • Better usability (User friendly)
  • There's a free version for schools (albeit more limited)
Cons
  • Costs money
  • User interface isn't as well designed as Google Classroom
  • Higher learning curve than Google Classroom
  • Known to cause issues and confusion when integrating with other Google applications
  • Gradebook tools aren't as robust/diverse as other options
  • Technically not an LMS (learning management system), and therefore does not have as many features/tools as other LMS's
